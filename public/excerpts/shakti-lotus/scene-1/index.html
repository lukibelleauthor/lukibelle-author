The Invitation

Desire does not knock. It waits to be noticed.

Larissa had not meant to linger on the photograph. Yet something in the woman’s eyes—faded, knowing, almost amused— refused to release her.

The letter arrived the same evening. No return address. No explanation. Only her name, written as though someone had practiced it for years.

She should not have opened it. But longing is a quiet persuader.
